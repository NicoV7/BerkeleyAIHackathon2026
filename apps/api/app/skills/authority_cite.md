# Authority Cite

**Move type:** ETHOS (credibility / authority)

## What it does
Anchor your claim to recognized expert consensus or a trusted institution so the
audience borrows that credibility. It shifts the question from "do you believe
me?" to "do you reject every expert in the field?".

## How to deploy it this turn
- Name the authority and the breadth of agreement ("the consensus of climate
  scientists", "every major medical body").
- State precisely what they conclude.
- Frame dissent as the burden: the opponent must out-argue the experts, not you.

## Example
"This isn't my opinion — it's the unanimous position of the National Academy of
Sciences, the WHO, and every peer-reviewed meta-analysis on the question. To
take the other side is to claim you know better than all of them at once."
