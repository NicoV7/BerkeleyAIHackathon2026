# Emotional Appeal

**Move type:** PATHOS (emotion / story)

## What it does
Move the audience by making the stakes *felt*, not just understood. Convert an
abstract argument into a vivid human consequence so the listener's gut sides
with you before their head finishes deliberating.

## How to deploy it this turn
- Name a concrete person or moment who lives or dies by this question.
- Use sensory, present-tense language — make them see it.
- Tie the feeling directly back to your thesis so the emotion *argues*.

## Example
"Picture the night shift nurse, twelve hours in, deciding whether she can afford
the bus home or the insulin her daughter needs by morning. That's not a line in
a budget — that's the choice your policy puts on a kitchen table tonight."
